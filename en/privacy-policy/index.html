6. Your Rights Over Your Personal Data

Under the Data Protection Act 2017 of Mauritius, you have the following rights:

6.1 Right of access

You may request a copy of all personal data we hold about you. This right is free of charge and we respond within 1 month.

6.2 Right of rectification

You may request the correction of inaccurate or incomplete data concerning you.

6.3 Right of erasure ("right to be forgotten")

You may request deletion of your data when: it is no longer necessary for its original purpose; you withdraw consent; you object successfully; or it has been processed unlawfully.

Limitation: We cannot erase data required by law (e.g. accounting records kept 7 years).

6.4 Right to object

You may object at any time to direct marketing processing (newsletter). The objection takes immediate effect.

6.5 Right to restriction of processing

You may request a temporary freeze on processing while verifying accuracy or contesting lawfulness.

6.6 Note on data portability

Mauritius-specific: The DPA 2017 does not explicitly provide a right to data portability (unlike the EU GDPR). Nevertheless, Pretty Tours will provide your data in a structured, readable format upon request where technically feasible.

13. Appendices

Appendix A: Legal Definitions (DPA 2017)

  • Personal data: Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person
  • Processing: Any operation on data (collection, recording, storage, modification, use, communication, erasure or destruction)
  • Data Controller: Entity determining purposes and means of processing (Pretty Tours Ltd)
  • Data Processor: Entity processing data on behalf of the controller
  • Consent: Freely given, specific, informed and unambiguous indication of agreement
  • Data breach: Security violation leading to accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration or unauthorised disclosure of data

Appendix B: Cookies

For the full list of cookies and retention periods, see our Cookie Policy.

Appendix C: Legal Bases under DPA 2017 (Mauritius)

  • Explicit consent of the data subject
  • Performance of a contract
  • Compliance with a legal obligation

  • Task in the public interest
  • Legitimate interests of the data controller (unless overridden by the data subject's rights)
